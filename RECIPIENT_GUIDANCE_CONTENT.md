# Recipient Guidance Content

## Purpose

This file contains the content that should be surfaced in the live portal after a resident selects a recipient package type.

The portal must explain:

- Why this recipient may matter
- What this recipient can do
- What this recipient cannot do
- Questions this package raises
- What response may be expected
- What the resident should do next

## Council / General Manager

Why this recipient may matter:
Council is the local authority positioned to develop a Bellbrook Flying-fox Camp Management Plan, seek grants, disclose consultation outcomes, and manage local infrastructure responses.

What this recipient can do:
Develop or commission a management plan; apply for relevant grants; disclose council records and consultation outputs; consider infrastructure and buffer actions.

What this recipient cannot do:
Treat meetings and letters as a substitute for documented outcomes.

Questions this package raises:
Will council develop a Bellbrook management plan? What outputs resulted from the August and September 2025 consultation activity? What responses were received from agencies? What funding applications have been made?

Expected response:
Possible responses include acknowledgement, referral to existing correspondence, a commitment to review, or a refusal with reasons.

Next step:
Keep the sent package and any reply. Record whether the reply answers the questions or only describes process activity.

## Transparency Statement

Resident authored:
- Personal testimony
- Personal observations
- Personal requested actions

Portal generated:
- Cover letter formatting
- Recipient guidance attachment
- Recipient-specific questions
- Submission guidance

The resident may edit, remove, or replace generated content before submission. Portal-generated material should not be treated as personal testimony unless the resident chooses to adopt it.
