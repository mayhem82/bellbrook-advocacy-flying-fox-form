# Recipient Intelligence Layer Specification

## Purpose

Residents should not need to understand media processes, parliamentary procedure, government administration, health systems, or Ombudsman processes.

The portal translates a resident account into recipient-specific context while preserving transparency and authorship.

## Package Structure

1. Community Impact Record
2. Resident Cover Letter
3. Recipient Briefing Attachment
4. Recipient Questions
5. Recipient Guidance
6. Transparency Statement
7. Submission Instructions

## Recipient Guidance Sections

### Why You Received This Package
Explains why the selected recipient may have an interest in the matter.

### What This Recipient Does
Defines the powers, responsibilities, and limitations of the recipient.

### Why This Recipient May Matter
Explains the relevance of the recipient to the issue.

### Questions This Package Raises
Recipient-specific questions generated from the evidence and context.

### What Response Should I Expect?
Provides realistic response expectations and outcomes.

### What To Do Next
Explains follow-up actions and response tracking.

## Transparency Requirements

Every generated package must clearly identify:

Resident Authored:
- Testimony
- Personal observations
- Personal requests

Portal Generated:
- Briefing notes
- Recipient guidance
- Recipient questions
- Submission instructions

Residents may edit, remove, or replace generated material before sending.

## Initial Recipient Categories

- Council
- Mayor / Councillors
- State MP
- Federal MP
- Environment Minister
- NSW Health
- NSW Ombudsman
- Media

## Design Principle

Simple for residents.
Transparent.
Recipient-specific.
Easy to understand.
Easy to act upon.
Preserves ownership of resident testimony.
