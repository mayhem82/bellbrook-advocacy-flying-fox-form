# Recipient Module Library

## Purpose

Reusable recipient-specific modules that accompany a resident's Bellbrook Flying Fox Community Impact Record.

Each module explains:
- Why the recipient matters
- What the recipient can do
- What the recipient cannot do
- Questions raised by the evidence
- Expected responses
- Next steps
- Transparency notes

---

# MEDIA MODULE

Purpose:
Help journalists understand why the issue may be newsworthy.

Can Do:
- Investigate
- Interview stakeholders
- Publish findings
- Request records

Cannot Do:
- Direct council action
- Create policy
- Resolve the issue directly

Key Question:
Why has six and a half years of process produced no documented outcome?

---

# NSW OMBUDSMAN MODULE

Purpose:
Support review of administrative conduct.

Can Do:
- Investigate administrative actions
- Request explanations
- Review records

Cannot Do:
- Force a management plan
- Replace council decision-making

Key Questions:
- Were available pathways properly considered?
- Why was the management plan declined?
- What became of consultation outputs?

---

# NSW HEALTH MODULE

Purpose:
Record health and wellbeing impacts.

Can Do:
- Record concerns
- Conduct assessments
- Provide advice

Cannot Do:
- Direct council policy
- Manage flying fox camps

Key Questions:
- Has any health assessment occurred?
- Will one occur?

---

# STATE MP MODULE

Purpose:
Enable parliamentary representation.

Can Do:
- Raise questions
- Contact agencies
- Advocate for constituents

Cannot Do:
- Direct agencies independently
- Override legislation

Key Questions:
- What action has government taken?
- What options remain available?

# FEDERAL MP MODULE

# ENVIRONMENT MINISTER MODULE

Purpose:
Seek environmental policy clarification.

Can Do:
- Provide policy responses
- Clarify mechanisms
- Direct departmental review

Cannot Do:
- Direct local council decisions in all circumstances

Key Questions:
- What mechanisms exist when a council refuses a management plan?
- Is Bellbrook correctly classified?

---

# COUNCIL MODULE

Purpose:
Direct engagement with local government.

Can Do:
- Develop management plans
- Seek grants
- Conduct management actions

Cannot Do:
- Ignore statutory obligations indefinitely

Key Questions:
- Will a management plan be developed?
- What outcomes resulted from consultation?
- What responses were received from agencies?
