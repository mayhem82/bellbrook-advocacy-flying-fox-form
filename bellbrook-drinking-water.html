What is already known

Several things are independently documented and can be cited with confidence while this section is built. Together they help explain a question residents reasonably ask: if Bellbrook has a water treatment plant, why has water still been trucked in?

  • Bellbrook's existing treatment plant, built in 2010, was constructed to remove arsenic from bore/groundwater, following elevated arsenic linked to historical mining contamination upstream. Council reports this specific problem is resolved and arsenic levels are kept within Australian Drinking Water Guidelines.
  • Water carting is a separate problem, caused by river water quality, not arsenic. In 2019–2020, drought caused the Macleay River to stop flowing; bushfires and then heavy rain followed, pushing iron and manganese levels too high in the river supply. Bellbrook and Willawarrin both had water trucked in for months, with the NSW Government funding $161,000 to cart around 10–13 kilolitres a day to each village, before both returned to river supply in June 2020.
  • This has happened more than once. Reporting on neighbouring Willawarrin describes it being forced into water carting "on numerous occasions" through repeated drought, fire, and flood cycles, because existing treatment could not reliably handle variable water quality — not a single 2019–2020 event.
  • New infrastructure is being built — but so far, confirmed mainly for Willawarrin and the main Kempsey supply, not Bellbrook. A $52.9 million project is building a new filtration plant for the Steuart McIntyre Dam and Sherwood Borefield network (construction from mid-2025, an 18-month build). Separately, Willawarrin is getting its own purpose-built $8 million treatment plant, under construction from mid-2025 and due for completion in early 2026, specifically designed to handle variable bore water quality during severe weather. No equivalent new plant for Bellbrook itself was found in public reporting at the time this page was built.

Whether Bellbrook has its own upgrade planned, and what Council's current position is, has not yet been confirmed directly with Council — this page does not claim there is no plan, only that none was found in what is publicly reported so far.
