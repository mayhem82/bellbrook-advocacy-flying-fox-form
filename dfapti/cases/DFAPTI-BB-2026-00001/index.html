What DFAPTI is (and isn't)

This is independent, source-verified research, not an official investigation, complaint, legal proceeding, or government report. It draws only on publicly available government/council material and independently cross-referenced search - every claim is classified as ACCEPTED (verified primary source), HELD (relevant but not fully verified), or an open gap (searched for, not found - logged honestly, not assumed). No finding of fault against any named person or organisation is made or implied anywhere on this site.

Sources are linked directly wherever available so anyone can check the original document themselves. This register is append-only and corrected in the open: if something here turns out to be wrong, a later entry says so - nothing is quietly edited or removed. See the portal's Legal & Privacy Notice for the full terms.

What this investigation is trying to establish

Establish the full, source-verified chronology of recurring drinking-water supply failures affecting Willawarrin and Bellbrook (trucked water episodes at least in 2020 and 2023, tied to high iron/manganese levels in the Macleay River during low-flow periods), and track the $8 million Willawarrin Water Treatment Plant intended as the permanent fix through to a confirmed completion or delay - resolving whether trucked water is still in use today.

Scope

In scope: council and NSW Government primary sources on water-supply incidents, dam/river extraction decisions, and the treatment plant's funding, construction, and commissioning status for Willawarrin and Bellbrook specifically. Out of scope for this case: general Kempsey Shire-wide water policy not tied to these two communities, and the separate Bellbrook flying-fox, asbestos, streets/roads, Armidale Road, and communications issues, which are tracked as their own matters.

E-001 — Kempsey Shire Council

Willawarrin and Bellbrook return to river supply after months of trucked-in water

Kempsey Shire Council's own media release confirming Willawarrin and Bellbrook were switched back to Macleay River supply on Thursday 18 June 2020, after 'many months' of having drinking water trucked in due to high iron and manganese levels making river water undrinkable. Council attributes the fix to a combination of bores and treatment processes.

Verification notes: Primary source: the council's own archived media release. Corroborated by contemporaneous secondary coverage (Macleay Argus, Port Macquarie News) reporting the same return-to-supply date and cause, though those are not themselves relied on as the primary source.

Kempsey Shire Council's own update confirming a second, separate trucked-water episode: from 27 September 2023 the Shire moved solely to Steuart McIntyre Dam water supply after Macleay River levels dropped below extraction limits. E-019 — Not yet identified

Open question: did the 2009 Bellbrook plant fail, or was 2020 a genuinely separate problem it was never designed for?

E-018 establishes Bellbrook had a dedicated treatment plant since 2009 for arsenic/heavy metal contamination. E-001 establishes Bellbrook still went through 'many months' of trucked water in 2020, attributed to iron and manganese, not arsenic. Two explanations are both consistent with the evidence so far and neither is yet confirmed: (a) the 2009 plant was built to treat arsenic/heavy metals specifically and was never designed to handle iron/manganese, so the 2020 crisis is a genuinely separate problem the existing plant could not address; or (b) the 2009 plant degraded, was decommissioned, or became inadequate over time and something has changed since 2009. Which of these is true has a direct bearing on whether Bellbrook's current water security depends on ageing single-purpose infrastructure, and it has not been established either way.

Verification notes: Logged as an open question rather than resolved either way, consistent with Contradiction Preservation - the two explanations are not merged into one assumed answer. Next step: find the 2009 plant's original design specification or scope (was iron/manganese ever in scope?), or find any reporting on the plant's current operating status.
