COMMUNITY DISCOVERY

Residents can suggest community concerns. Public cards show concern titles and independent-account counts, not names or private details.

How this works

This page is for community-led discovery. Residents can raise concerns without the portal deciding the next dedicated section in advance. Suggestions are grouped only when independent resident accounts appear to describe the same concern, place, pattern, authority pathway, or impact.

No public naming.

The public board should display concern titles, count of independent resident accounts, first suggested date, status, and short summary only. Names, addresses, contact details, and full account text are not displayed publicly.

Status pathway

  • Community Suggestion: One or more resident accounts name a concern.
  • Growing Community Interest: Multiple independent resident accounts appear to describe the same concern.
  • Community Review: Accounts are checked for consistency, location, timeframe, evidence, and requested outcome.
  • Candidate for Dedicated Section: The concern is defined clearly enough to justify a build pathway.
  • Dedicated Bellbrook Section: The concern is promoted into its own public-facing section.

Promotion criteria

No concern is promoted automatically. Promotion depends on the strength of the accumulated community record.

  • Number of independent resident accounts.
  • Consistency between accounts.
  • Supporting photos, documents, dates, or locations.
  • Duration or recurrence of the concern.
  • Potential community impact.
  • Whether the concern can be defined as one coherent matter.
