About this project

NSW Community Resilience Portals is a shared framework used to help residents in different communities turn local experiences, concerns, and observations into clear records that can be saved, shared, and followed up. Each community has its own portal because each sits in a different local government area, with its own council, its own issues, and its own community.

How this works

Residents write accounts in their own words, on their own community's portal. The portal organises those accounts into clear records. The records can be saved, sent, archived by email where available, and grouped with similar records to show patterns within that community.

Every community portal shares the same underlying methodology. If you would like to understand how the framework reviews community records, groups similar issues, and separates different types of evidence, read How Community Records Work.

About community

Each portal belongs to the community it serves. Every resident account remains the author's own record. No portal speaks for residents. Each one helps residents organise, preserve, and share their own experiences in a clear and consistent way.

Planned next stage

Future updates will allow residents to keep track of responses received from councils, government agencies, organisations, and other recipients, across every community portal. This will help build a clear history of what was submitted, when it was acknowledged, and what happened next.
