About this portal

The Bellbrook Advocacy Portal is a community evidence and submission system for Bellbrook residents. It helps people turn lived experience into clear records, direct those records to the right recipients, and keep copies for follow-up.

Why it has been built

Small communities often experience problems long before those problems become visible in official systems. Residents may report issues separately, receive unclear responses, or be left without a shared record of what has been said, who was contacted, and what was done.

This portal exists so Bellbrook can hold its own record.

How investigations are conducted

The portal uses a method-based local forensic process. It separates resident account, evidence held, inference, verification needed, authority pathway, and action sought. It does not pretend to be a lawyer, regulator, doctor, scientist, or expert witness.

About community

The portal is not owned by an institution. It is a community tool. Each resident account remains the resident's own account. The portal does not replace personal voice. It helps protect, organise, and carry that voice into a form that can be submitted, retained, compared, and followed up.

About yield

The purpose of the portal is yield for the community: clearer understanding, stronger records, better routing, and the ability for residents to name what is happening in a form that is harder to dismiss.

Promotion model

Discovery is not a permanent issue number. The current discovery slot collects local accounts. When enough accounts identify the same issue, the discovery slot can be promoted into a dedicated active issue module. The next discovery slot then moves forward to the next issue number.

Planned next stage

Response tracking is planned. The current portal does not yet store or track responses. It is being structured so submissions can later be logged, followed up, and classified.
