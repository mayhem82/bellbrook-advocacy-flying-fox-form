What is actually happening with the flying foxes and Camphor Laurel at Taylors Arm.

What is actually happening at Taylors Arm

Taylors Arm is a small village in the Nambucca Valley hinterland, home to the Pub With No Beer, a nationally known tourist destination made famous by the Slim Dusty song. A colony of grey-headed flying-foxes has established itself in the village, roosting in Camphor Laurel trees near homes and the council-managed reserve. This page sets out what is publicly known before you record your own account.

The bats are protected — and the trees they roost in are a declared weed

The grey-headed flying-fox is a protected species under NSW and federal law. The colony cannot be killed or dispersed by force without going through a lawful process. That is a real legal constraint, the same one that applies at other flying-fox camps.

The trees many of the Taylors Arm flying-foxes are roosting in — Camphor Laurel — are a different matter. Camphor Laurel is classified as a priority weed under the NSW Biosecurity Act 2015, and landholders (including councils, for land they manage) have a general biosecurity duty to manage the risk it poses. That creates a genuine tension: the roosting habitat is something council and landholders are otherwise expected to control, while the animals roosting in it are protected.

What the species protection does NOT prevent:

  • Developing a formal management approach for the Taylors Arm colony
  • Assessing Camphor Laurel management options consistent with flying-fox camp management rules
  • Creating buffer zones between roosting trees and homes
  • Applying for government grant funding to help pay for management actions
  • Sustained formal contact with state and federal agencies about the site

What has been raised with Council so far

Advocacy research prepared for this page indicates that a Taylors Arm resident raised concerns about the flying-fox colony and Camphor Laurel roosting trees at a Nambucca Valley Council meeting held in Taylors Arm in March 2023. This portal has not independently obtained a copy of the full meeting minutes and has not confirmed what follow-up, if any, took place after that meeting. If you have a copy of the relevant council record, it can help strengthen the community record described on this page.

Verification note: Council meeting agendas and minutes are public documents available through Nambucca Valley Council. Anyone wanting to check the details above is encouraged to request or review those records directly rather than rely solely on this summary.

Why this matters for Taylors Arm's tourism economy

The Pub With No Beer is a well-known regional tourist destination, and the council-managed campground sits opposite it. Residents and visitors affected by odour, noise, mess, or reduced amenity near roosting trees may reasonably consider this relevant to the village's tourism value, alongside the health and daily-life impacts described elsewhere on this page. This portal has not independently measured any economic impact; it is included here as a plausible community concern, not a proven finding.

What would actually help

The single most important thing that needs to happen is that Nambucca Valley Council develops a documented management approach for the Taylors Arm flying-fox colony and Camphor Laurel roosting trees, in consultation with the community and relevant NSW agencies. That approach would:

  • Give council a clear, documented basis for management actions at the site
  • Help clarify how species protection and weed-control duties interact at this specific site
  • Open a pathway to state government grant funding, where available
  • Create a documented framework that agencies and residents can follow up against

Until that exists, resident accounts documented in formal submissions are the evidence that helps make the case for action. Your experience matters, whether or not you have raised it with anyone before.
