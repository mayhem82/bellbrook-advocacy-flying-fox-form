What is already known

One thing is independently documented and can be cited with confidence while this section is built: solar feed-in tariffs across NSW have fallen sharply over the past few years. The current statewide benchmark range is roughly 3.4–6.5 cents per kilowatt-hour, well down from the more generous rates — often around 20 cents per kilowatt-hour — that many retailers offered a few years ago. Some NSW distributors have also introduced export charges (a "sun tax") on solar sent to the grid during the middle of the day.

As far as residents are aware, this decline has affected Willawarrin households with solar power directly: an export rate falling from around 21c/kWh to around 5c/kWh, exporting roughly four units of solar power for every one used, while still paying close to $220 a month. This portal has not independently verified these specific figures for any household — they are recorded here as a resident account, consistent with the statewide trend, not as a confirmed community-wide finding.

Planned purpose

This section is being built to help Willawarrin residents with solar power record their own experience of feed-in tariff changes — export rates, bill impact, and what retailers or the network operator have said — along with location notes, evidence descriptions, who was contacted, what reply was received, and what action is being asked for.

The form must separate resident accounts, observations, evidence held, verification needed, and requested action.
